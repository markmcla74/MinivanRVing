Healthy Gut Routine

We tend to take our health for granted until something goes wrong. In my case, I was diagnosed with Acute Severe Ulcerative Colitis in February 2023. Ulcerative Colitis is a miserable digestive disease. I was hospitalized twice, and from January - March 2023, I had lost so much weight that I was just skin and bones. Thankfully, during my second hospitalization at the end of March 2023, I received an infusion of Remicade. This medicine worked miraculously on me. Within hours I felt like a dark cloud that had been keeping me down, had just floated away. I was able to recover in just a couple of months. By July 2023, my colon looked almost normal. During my recovery, I also implemented a healthy gut routine. The tables below show my healthy gut routine, which has kept me free from any flares since my first infusion of Remicade. This information might be helpful to someone else who is suffering from Ulcerative Colitis, or anyone who is interested in improving his or her gut health.

UPDATE JAN/FEB/MARCH 2024: My fecal calprotectin level is elevated, and I have symptoms of a mild flare. I strayed from my normal routine during the Christmas and New Years Holidays, and now I'm paying for it. In addition, my recent infusion of Inflectra in early February had no effect at reducing my symptoms. Perhaps the Inflectra contained the flare to just the end of my colon, but that's unclear. Nevertheless, I have hope! I am receiving 12 hyperbaric oxygen treatments during the month of March. I will provide an update soon!

UPDATE JAN 2025: The hyperbaric oxygen treatments reduced my symptoms, but not enough to get me out of my flare. Once I took prednisone, the flare went away. However, the hyperbaric oxygen treatments were not a failure. The treatments just weren't strong enough as a sole treatment. I mentioned to my gastroenterologist that I would eventually like to discontinue Inflectra infusions. (Inflectra is a powerful medicine, and there is an increased risk of cancer with long term use.) I suggested that I would like to use mesalamine enemas and hyperbaric oxygen treatments as a maintainence therapy. This would discourage the return of ulcerative colitis flares by working both locally with the enemas, and systemically with the hyperbaric oxygen. My doctor isn't opposed to this plan! So perhaps I'll try this in 2026, or earlier. I've been free from a flare since April 2024, and I'm still receiving Inflectra infusions. (Although I flared in early 2024, I didn't develop antibodies to the Inflectra. So I'm still receiving the infusions.) I'm feeling great!


Diet
Breakfast:
Steel Cut Oatmeal with Kefir & Blueberries (Kefir is homemade from raw unpasteurized whole milk)
Chicken Bone Broth -Brodino brand, purchased at farmers’ market. Drink with breakfast.
Lunch:
Kefir Smoothie (Usually made with frozen bananas, frozen strawberries, and frozen blueberries)
Scrambled Eggs (Microwave farmers’ market eggs in Pyrex dish.)
Avocado and mild salsa
Tortillas, Rice, or Farmers’ Market Sourdough Bread
Dinner:
Bison Burgers or Fish
Steamed Vegetables
Avocado and mild salsa
Tortillas, Rice, or Farmers’ market Sourdough Bread


Medicines and Supplements
Mesalamine 2.4 grams/day
Remicade/Inflectra infusions -Every 8 weeks
Vitamin D
Vitamin B
Multivitamin
Fish Oil


Lifestyle
Install pur water filter in sink in garage. Store 2 Nalgene bottles of filtered water in refrigerator
Keep a Nalgene bottle with filtered water near me as much as possible
Drink large glass of filtered water first thing in the morning
Exercise regularly as described below. 5 days a week is my goal. But I don't stress if I don't meet this goal.
    Exercise Option 1: Total body workout: 30 minutes of isometric and body weight exercises. -But I take very few breaks. It’s a miserable 30 min
    Exercise Option 2: Exercise bike: 20 minutes. Alternate 90 sec fairly easy, 90 sec difficult, for 20 min
    Exercise Option 3: Home Workout Routine: Typically I choose a P90X, Rushfit, or P90X3 workout.
    Exercise Option 4: Other Activities: Hike Mt Baldy from Manker Flats, Basketball, P90X2 Yoga, or 20 min of Flowfit
Sleep: Usually 7.5 – 8 hrs, and usually 20 min nap when I return home from work
No caffeine
1 or 2 glasses of wine per month maximum. No other alcohol
Go out to eat as little as possible. Usually 1 time per month
